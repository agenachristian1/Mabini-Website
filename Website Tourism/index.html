About Mabini

Mabini, officially the Municipality of Mabini, is a 2nd class municipality in the province of Batangas, Philippines. Known as the "Diving Mecca of the Philippines," it boasts some of the most beautiful dive sites, pristine beaches, and scenic mountains in the country.

Located on the southwestern tip of Batangas, Mabini is home to Anilao, the birthplace of scuba diving in the Philippines, attracting divers from around the world with its rich marine biodiversity.

How to get here

  1. From Manila, take the STAR Tollway towards Batangas City.
  2. Exit at Lipa or Batangas City, then follow signs to Mabini/Anilao.
  3. Public buses and vans (UV Express) also run regular routes ‚Äî check schedules in advance.
  4. Travel time: Approximately 2.5-3 hours from Manila.

Travel Tips

  • Best to visit during weekdays to avoid crowds
  • Bring cash as some establishments don't accept cards
  • Check weather forecasts before planning outdoor activities
  • Book accommodations in advance during peak season (Nov-May)

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to visit?

Dry season (November to May) is generally best for diving and island hopping. However, early weekdays can be quieter even during peak season.

Are there guided hikes available?

Yes ‚Äî local guides offer half-day and full-day hikes, often including sunrise or sunset options. Guides can be arranged through the tourism office.

Is Mabini suitable for families?

Definitely ‚Äî there are family-friendly resorts, calm beaches, and activities for all ages. Many resorts offer kids' clubs and family packages.

Do I need a diving license to dive?

For scuba diving, yes. But many dive shops offer Discover Scuba Diving programs for beginners. Snorkeling requires no certification.

What should I pack?

Sun protection, swimwear, comfortable shoes for hiking, light clothing, waterproof bag, and any personal medications. Don't forget your camera!

Are there ATM machines available?

Limited ATMs are available in the town proper. It's recommended to bring enough cash, especially when visiting remote islands.
